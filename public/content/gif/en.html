SPLIT SCREEN & GIF

Fleeting set of island languages, the GIF is a fragmentary and fragmented excerpt from the real world. Its punctual shapes are constantly thwarted by the minute loop of time. The experiences of this mode of reading change diametrically our relation to narration, then to the consent that is usually or not accorded to movement and duration. Beyond its scale and its virtual exploitation, the GIF is a format allowing a semantic compression, which one acquires and that one receives instantly. This spatio-temporal loop has emerged through the practice of filmmakers such as Chris Marker and Alain Resnais — experimenting with back-and-forth screenings, multiple and repeated interpretations at the same time or in identical places : reversals that apply throughout the entire scope of a work. It is a question of understanding what the uncertain repetition of an equivalent and fugitive action means today. The GIF format would thus be a leitmotiv in which the responsibility to fail is assumed, to never reach anywhere — except in a constant, almost maniacal re-creation. On the ultimate film set, in the heart of a set of superimposed layers : the vision vanishes and reborns, a retinal persistence whose casual attitude is in the grip of renunciation — it imprints its mark on our eyes.
