The Hotel des Berges de la Loire - cinematographic situation

Cineplastic stroll proposed within temporarily invested hotel rooms. The imagery of the cinema is marked by many iconic places that have lastingly marked its history and ours — the hotels are among these most mythical platforms in the sense of Barthésien, visible through such films as The Last Men by Friedrich-Wilhelm Murnau , Marcel Carné's Northern Hotel, Alfred Hitchcock's Psycho, Alain Resnais's Last Year at Marienbad, Ingmar Bergman's Silence, Alice in the Cities of Wim Wenders, Chantal Akerman's Anna's Appointments , Nostalghia by Andrey Tarkovsky, Mala Noche by Gus Van Sant, David Lynch's Twin Peaks, 2046 by Wong Kar-wai, and Tomita Katsuya's recent Bangkok Nites — all of which depict the figure of the hotel in all its forms, all the times and according to the comings and goings of a gallery of very singular characters, often in prey or in search of solitude, introspection.

This project focus on the question of looking at what "makes film" — to live it simultaneously, to immerse itself in its material, a cinephile word — then to observe what constitutes it plastically in its contextualized forms except "white cube", where the creations can incarnate otherwise. The long corridors of the hotel invite to wander in what is left in the cinema when you remove the experience of the projection to give to see the anatomy — a constellation in "split screen", polyvisual or polyptych, of this what is the "totality" of the seventh of the arts. This navigation progressively unveil a scripted archipelago, visual and performed in which come to incarnate several characters, concepts, objects, images and words : the search for a cinematics.
